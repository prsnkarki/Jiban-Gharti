Client Questions

You Ask, I Answer

How much does a project cost?

Every project is scoped individually — no flat rates, no guesswork. Pricing depends on scope, complexity, and timeline. After a quick discovery conversation I'll give you a clear quote with zero surprises. Logo projects typically start from NPR 15,000 and brand identity systems from NPR 35,000.

How long does a typical project take?

A logo design takes 5–10 days. A full brand identity system is 3–4 weeks. UI/UX projects vary based on number of screens. I'll always give you a clear timeline before we begin, and I don't rush to hit it — I hit it because I planned well.

Do you work with international clients?

Absolutely. I work fully remotely and have experience with clients across different time zones. Communication happens via email, WhatsApp, or video call — whatever suits you. Payments can be arranged in USD, EUR, or NPR.

What do you need from me to get started?

Just a brief — tell me what your brand is, who it's for, what you love and hate, and any references you find interesting. I'll send you a simple brief template to fill out. The clearer the brief, the better the outcome. No design jargon needed — just be honest about what you want.

How many revision rounds are included?

Most projects include 2–3 structured revision rounds. I present concepts with clear rationale, gather your feedback, then refine. In my experience, most projects don't need more than 2 rounds if the brief is solid — but I won't leave you with something you're not proud of.
